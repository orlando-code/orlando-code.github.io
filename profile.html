Biography

Hello! I'm Orlando: computational physicist & data scientist by training, conservationist by heart.

After several years studying physics and being gradually won over by the impressive power of modern computational methods, particularly machine learning, I've taken the leap to pursue my passion: marine conservation.

In 2023 I started a PhD at the University of Cambridge, UK, with a coral-loving supervisor. I'm working to apply my computational skills to maximise the effectiveness and efficiency of conservation efforts. As always, I'm on the look-out for collaborations and potential co-supervisors...

I've set myself the challenge of predicting global coral coverage – present and future – using multimodal data including bathymetry and oceanographic conditions from high-resolution CMIP6 runs. There's plenty more work to be done and directions to explore: I'd love to hear any suggestions on where to take it!
